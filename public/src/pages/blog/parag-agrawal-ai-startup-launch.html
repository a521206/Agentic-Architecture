Former Twitter CEO Parag Agrawal Launches AI Startup with $30M Seed Funding

August 15, 2025 4 min read

San Francisco, August 15, 2025 – Parag Agrawal, the former CEO of Twitter, has officially launched Parallel Web Systems Inc., an AI startup aimed at revolutionizing how AI agents interact with the internet.

The two-year-old company, known simply as Parallel, announced its debut on August 14, 2025, after securing $30 million in a seed funding round led by Khosla Ventures, with backing from Index Ventures, First Round Capital, and other prominent investors.

Revolutionizing AI-Web Interactions

Parallel's cloud platform is designed to power autonomous AI agents, enabling them to conduct real-time web research with unprecedented efficiency. The platform offers eight AI research engines, with its flagship Ultra8x outperforming OpenAI's GPT-5 by over 10% on benchmarks like BrowseComp and DeepResearch Bench.

These engines provide APIs for tasks such as:

  • Retrieving code from GitHub for programming assistants
  • Compiling competitive product data for retailers
  • Providing confidence scores and citations for data verification
  • Supporting customizable outputs like spreadsheets for product reviews

From Twitter to AI Infrastructure

Agrawal, who was ousted as Twitter CEO in October 2022 following Elon Musk's $44 billion acquisition of the platform (now X), has pivoted to building infrastructure for what he predicts will be a future dominated by AI-driven web traffic.

"The internet needs to evolve for machines, not just humans."

— Parag Agrawal, CEO of Parallel Web Systems

Current Traction and Future Plans

Parallel's platform already processes millions of research tasks daily for early adopters, including fast-growing AI companies. The startup plans to expand its capabilities, enabling AI agents to handle team-level workloads in hours and adapt to dynamic webpage changes.

Parallel is actively hiring engineers to develop its "parallel web" optimized for machine-generated traffic, signaling strong growth ambitions in the AI infrastructure space.
